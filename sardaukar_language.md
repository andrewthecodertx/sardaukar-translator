Sardaukar Language: Linguistic Characteristics for Programmatic Translation

---

## **Overview**

Sardaukar is a constructed, highly efficient language designed for the elite soldiers in the *Dune* universe. Its primary features are brevity, harsh phonology, and ritualistic spoken forms. The language is not fully developed but is based on aggressive compression and modification of English, with influences from Norse and throat singing traditions[^1][^4].

---

## **Core Linguistic Features**

- **Extreme Compression:**
    - Remove ~75% of English sounds and syllables.
    - Eliminate unnecessary words, keeping only the essential meaning[^1].
- **Agglutinative Morphology:**
    - Add suffixes for pluralization and verb forms (e.g., -e, -r, -de, -zi).
    - Each suffix represents a single grammatical function.
- **Sound System:**
    - Simplify vowels to “eh,” “ah,” “ee,” or “oo.”
    - Use harsh, guttural consonants.
    - Frequent use of double consonants (geminates)—pronounce each twice[^2].
    - Employ throat singing or overtone singing for ritualistic effect[^1].
- **Word Order:**
    - Subject-Verb-Object (SVO), similar to English.
- **Lexicon:**
    - Core vocabulary is a compressed, modified form of English, with invented words as needed.
    - Pronouns and articles are minimized or omitted for efficiency.
- **Prosody and Delivery:**
    - Speech is fast, clipped, and may layer sounds for dramatic effect.
    - Ritual chants use deep, resonant, and sometimes simultaneous tones[^1][^8].

---

## **Text-to-Text Translation Rules**

1. **Strip Down Input:**
    - Remove auxiliary verbs, articles, and modifiers unless essential.
    - Retain only the core subject, verb, and object.
2. **Apply Agglutinative Suffixes:**
    - Pluralize with -e (consonant stem) or -r (vowel stem).
    - Infinitive verbs use -de; third-person singular uses -zi.
3. **Compress and Modify Sounds:**
    - Replace vowels with “eh,” “ah,” “ee,” or “oo.”
    - Collapse consonant clusters; double up harsh consonants where possible.
4. **Reconstruct for SVO Order:**
    - Ensure output follows Subject-Verb-Object structure.

---

## **Text-to-Speech Guidelines**

- **Phonetic Emphasis:**
    - Pronounce double consonants distinctly.
    - Use guttural, low-pitched resonance.
    - For ritualistic lines, incorporate overtone/throat singing techniques.
- **Speed and Rhythm:**
    - Deliver lines quickly, with abrupt stops.
    - Minimize intonation variation except in chants.
